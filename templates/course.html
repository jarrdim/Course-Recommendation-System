EXPLORE COURSES

Software Engineering

Software engineering focuses on the design, development, testing, and maintenance of software systems. It involves programming, problem-solving, and understanding algorithms and data structures.

A good foundation in mathematics and computer science is essential. Universities offering software engineering may require specific subjects like mathematics and computer studies, and some may require a minimum KCSE grade.

Medicine

Medicine involves the study of the human body, diseases, and medical treatments. It leads to careers as doctors, surgeons, or other medical professionals.

Usually, a strong performance in biology, chemistry, and physics is necessary. Some universities might have additional requirements, such as a minimum grade in KCSE and specific subjects.

Aerospace

Aerospace engineering deals with the design and development of aircraft, spacecraft, and related systems. It involves aerodynamics, materials science, and propulsion systems.

Strong mathematics and physics background is crucial. Specific universities may have additional subject requirements, and some may require a minimum grade in KCSE.

Law

Law studies focus on understanding legal principles, systems, and practices. It prepares students for careers as lawyers, judges, or legal professionals.

A good background in humanities and social sciences is often required. English and history might be important subjects. Additionally, some universities may have specific KCSE grade requirements.

Music

Music courses cover various aspects of music theory, performance, and composition. It includes the study of musical history, different genres, and practical skills.

While specific requirements may vary, a background in music, including performance and theory, may be beneficial. Some universities may have auditions or portfolios as part of the application process.

Engineering

Engineering is a broad field that involves the application of scientific and mathematical principles to design and build structures, devices, systems, and processes. Specializations include civil, mechanical, electrical, and more.

Typically, you need a strong background in mathematics and physics. Specific universities may have additional subject requirements, and some may require a minimum grade in KCSE.
